# Attribution & Framework Credits

The Founder Jam curriculum draws on established frameworks from the fields of strategy, product, and customer development. We are grateful to the researchers and authors whose work underpins the workshop experience.

---

## Core Frameworks

**Good Strategy / Bad Strategy**
Richard Rumelt. The strategy kernel (diagnosis, guiding policy, coherent actions) is referenced throughout the hypothesis and positioning sessions.

**Jobs-to-be-Done Theory**
Clayton Christensen, Bob Moesta, and Tony Ulwick. The JTBD framework powers ICP definition and customer motivation work across both tracks.

**Double Diamond Design Process**
British Design Council. The diverge-then-converge structure is used in hypothesis development and prototype sessions.

**Customer Development**
Steve Blank. The "get out of the building" philosophy and hypothesis-driven validation mindset inform the 30-day validation plan and prototype sessions.

**Value Proposition Canvas**
Alexander Osterwalder and Yves Pigneur (Strategyzer). The customer profile and value map structure is used in positioning and offer development.

**Business Model Canvas**
Alexander Osterwalder and Yves Pigneur (Strategyzer). Referenced in go-to-market and distribution strategy sessions.

**Hyper-Congruent Lead Magnets / Free Micro-Tools as Lead Magnets**
Greg Isenberg (Late Checkout / Startup Ideas Podcast, 2024-2025). Content-specific lead magnets, micro-tool concepts, validation cascade methodology. Referenced in lead magnet selection sessions.

James Dickerson (The Vibe Marketer / BoringMarketing, 2024-2025). AI-first asset creation methodology, lead magnet velocity and rotation principles. Referenced in content workflow sessions.

## Workshop Design

Founder Jam was designed and produced by StealthX (https://stealthx.co/) in partnership with Innovate Charlotte (https://www.innovatecharlotte.org/).

The workshop format -- one dedicated facilitator per startup, full-day working session, tangible deliverables -- draws on design sprint methodology pioneered by Google Ventures (Jake Knapp, John Zeratsky, Braden Kowitz).
