# Facilitator Playbook

Master reference for Founder Jam facilitators. Covers both Product Track and Growth Track. Read this before every event.

---

## 1. Vision & Role

Founder Jam exists to strengthen the North Carolina startup ecosystem by giving early-stage founders a high-impact, one-day experience to accelerate their journey. This is not about theoretical knowledge -- it is about doing real work.

### What You Are

- The dedicated guide for one startup throughout the entire day
- A provocateur who challenges assumptions and asks the questions founders avoid
- A systems thinker who pushes beyond tactics to consider unit economics, distribution, and sustainable growth loops
- An accountability partner who keeps teams moving and producing tangible outputs

### What You Are Not

- A teacher delivering a lecture
- A consultant solving problems for them
- A validator confirming whatever the founder believes
- A technical co-founder building their product

**Key mindset:** Your job is to ask the questions that surface "aha!" moments and keep teams moving with velocity. Let them struggle before you help. The struggle is where learning happens.

---

## 2. Ground Rules

Set these at the start of the day and reinforce them throughout:

- Be fully engaged. Laptops open for research and tools, not email.
- There are no bad ideas during divergence -- we converge later.
- If you're a talker, leave space. If you're a listener, lean in.
- Use "Yes, and..." instead of "but."
- Challenge founders, don't validate them. Comfortable founders don't learn.
- Let them struggle before helping. Facilitation is not rescue.
- Speed over perfection. Working prototype beats theoretical perfection.
- Times are a guide. The lead facilitator may flex the agenda based on momentum.

## 4. Session-by-Session Guide: Product Track

### Activity 1: Competitive Landscape Sprint
**Duration:** 90 minutes (9:20-10:50)
**Goal:** Replace assumptions with evidence. Identify high-leverage opportunities by understanding the competitive landscape.

**Steps:**
1. Conduct SWOT analysis (10 min) -- where do they think they stand today?
2. Identify 3-5 competitors and build battle cards in Miro -- always include the status quo
3. Revisit SWOT with new competitive data
4. Synthesize insights: where are the real opportunities?

**Battle Card Fields:**
Name, positioning, ICP, pricing, onboarding/switching costs, integrations, strengths, weaknesses, case studies, "how we win"

**Facilitator Prompts:**
- "What job is the customer hiring today without you?"
- "Where are switching costs and friction the highest?"
- "What's your unfair advantage the big players can't copy soon?"
- "Who are you losing deals to, and why?"

**Tools:** Miro battle card template, Perplexity, Claude, Crunchbase, company sites

**What to Watch For:**
- Founders who say "we have no competitors" -- the status quo is always a competitor
- Battle cards that list features but miss positioning -- push for the "how we win" column
- Competitive analysis that ignores distribution -- ask how each competitor acquires customers, not just what they sell

---

### Activity 1 Read-Out (Lightning)
**Duration:** 25 minutes (10:50-11:15)
- Each team shares one key insight from their competitive analysis (2-3 min per team)
- Facilitators and peers give one pointed question or observation
- Lead facilitator synthesizes patterns across teams

---

### Activity 2: Hypothesis Engine
**Duration:** 45 minutes (11:15-12:00)
**Goal:** Choose a small number of high-leverage bets to test. Replace vague intentions with falsifiable hypotheses.

**Format:**
- Double Diamond: diverge on options, converge on top 2-3 based on effort/impact
- Use crisp "If ___ then ___ by ___" framing (include success metric and timebox)
- Sense-check against the team's capabilities and timeline

**Hypothesis Templates:**
- "If we [change/action], [target metric] will [change by X] by [date] because [why]."
- "We believe [ICP] will pay $Y/month for [outcome]. We will validate by [experiment]."

**Facilitator Prompts:**
- "Is this falsifiable? What would prove you wrong?"
- "What's the smallest experiment that tests this?"
- "If this fails, what's the cost -- time, money, reputation?"
- "Which hypothesis unlocks the most optionality if it's true?"

**What to Watch For:**
- Hypotheses that are too broad to test in 30 days
- Founders confusing features with bets -- push for outcome framing
- Teams that converge too fast without considering alternatives

---

### Activity 3: Prototype Launcher
**Duration:** 135 minutes (1:15-3:30)
**Goal:** Build the cheapest test that yields the fastest learning.

**Example Prototypes (by use case):**
- Service/journey: map onboarding to first value; identify moments to remove friction
- GTM: cold-start outreach + 5-step nurture, landing page, offer test
- Pricing/packaging: tiers, usage metric, CAC/LTV quick math
- Sales story: 6-8 slide narrative for partners or prospects
- Feature: clickable mock or AI-generated demo (Lovable.dev) -- not production code

**Facilitator Mantras:**
- "Be ready to throw it away if it teaches you the wrong path."
- "Right problem beats shiny solution."
- "Done beats perfect."

**Facilitator Prompts:**
- "What's the riskiest assumption in this prototype?"
- "Who will you show this to in the next 48 hours?"
- "What would make you kill this idea? Define that now."
- "Can you build a version of this that teaches you something by Friday?"

**Tools:** Miro, sticky notes, Carrd.co, Lovable.dev, Replit, Figma, Claude

---

### Demos & Feedback
**Duration:** 30 minutes (3:30-4:00)
- Each startup demos their prototype (3-5 min per team)
- Structured feedback: one thing that works, one sharpening question
- Lead facilitator draws out patterns across teams

---

### Commitments
**Duration:** 30 minutes (4:00-4:30)
- Each founder writes 3 commitments: 2 days, 2 weeks, 2 months
- Each reads aloud to the group -- public commitment increases follow-through
- Facilitators witness and note the commitments for follow-up

## 5. Session-by-Session Guide: Growth Track

### Block 1: Define & Discover (8AM - 11AM)

---

#### Session 1.1: ICP Definition
**Duration:** ~60 minutes
**Goal:** Replace vague assumptions with specific, actionable customer profiles.

**What Founders Are Doing:**
1. Starting with current customers (if any) or target personas
2. Applying Jobs-to-be-Done framework: "When _____, I want to _____, so I can _____"
3. Identifying 2-3 distinct segments based on pain intensity, willingness to pay, and accessibility
4. Prioritizing one primary ICP for the day's focus

**Facilitator Prompts:**
- "Who is already solving this problem without you? How?"
- "Where does this person spend their time online?"
- "What job are they hiring your product to do?"
- "What's the trigger event that makes them start looking for a solution?"

**What to Watch For:**
- "We can help anyone" -- this is the most common trap; push for specificity on who is the BEST fit
- ICP defined by demographics, not problems -- flip to problem-first thinking
- No trigger event identified -- if they don't know when buyers become buyers, the ICP isn't real yet
- Multiple ICPs with equal priority -- insist they pick one for today; they can revisit later

**Tools:** Miro ICP canvas, Claude for persona research

---

#### Session 1.2: Competitive Growth Analysis
**Duration:** ~60 minutes
**Goal:** Identify high-leverage growth opportunities by analyzing how competitors acquire and nurture customers.

**What Founders Are Doing:**
1. Identifying 3-5 direct and adjacent competitors
2. Using SEMrush, Ahrefs, or similar to analyze top organic keywords, content gaps, backlink sources, and paid search strategy
3. Building battle cards: strengths, weaknesses, channels, content types, CTAs
4. Synthesizing insights: "Where can we win that they're not focused on?"

**Facilitator Prompts:**
- "What content are they NOT creating that their audience needs?"
- "Where are their blind spots in distribution?"
- "What channels are they neglecting?"
- "How could you out-teach or out-content them in a niche?"

**What to Watch For:**
- Analysis focused on product features instead of growth channels -- redirect to how they acquire customers
- Battle cards without a "how we win" synthesis
- Founders who haven't identified the status quo as a competitor

**Tools:** SEMrush, Ahrefs, SimilarWeb, Miro battle card template

---

#### Session 1.3: Distribution Strategy
**Duration:** ~45 minutes
**Goal:** Choose 1-2 primary channels and map out tactical first steps.

**What Founders Are Doing:**
1. Mapping ICP to channels (where they consume content and make decisions)
2. Assessing channel fit: audience match, competition level, time to traction, cost
3. Selecting primary channel and one backup
4. Drafting channel-specific first actions ("Publish 2 LinkedIn posts/week for 30 days")

**Facilitator Prompts:**
- "Where does your ICP go when they have this problem?"
- "Which channel can you own with consistency?"
- "What's your realistic publishing cadence for 30 days?"
- "How will you measure if this channel is working?"

**What to Watch For:**
- Choosing too many channels -- founders need focus, not breadth, for the 30-day plan
- Channel selection not connected to ICP research -- ask them to show the link
- "We'll figure it out later" on channel-specific tactics -- nail it down now

**Tools:** Channel selection matrix (Miro), competitive research from Session 1.2

---

### Block 2: Design (11AM - 2:30PM)

---

#### Session 2.1: Offer Development & Positioning
**Duration:** ~60 minutes
**Goal:** Create a compelling lead magnet and clear value proposition.

**What Founders Are Doing:**
1. Identifying the biggest pain point or knowledge gap for their ICP
2. Brainstorming lead magnet formats: template, checklist, calculator, audit, mini-course, swipe file
3. Choosing one that's valuable AND quick to create
4. Drafting an outline or first version using AI tools

**Facilitator Prompts:**
- "What's the one thing your ICP would pay for if packaged right?"
- "What question do they ask Google before finding you?"
- "Can you create this in the next 60 minutes?"
- "What format makes them say 'I need this now'?"

**What to Watch For:**
- Lead magnet that doesn't connect to the product -- should create a natural path to the sales conversation
- Scope creep on the lead magnet -- keep it achievable in 60-90 minutes
- Positioning statement that leads with features, not outcomes

**Tools:** Claude for content generation, Gamma or Canva for design, Google Docs for outlines

---

#### Lunch + AI Panel: "AI-Native Growth: What's Working"
**Duration:** ~90 minutes (includes lunch)
**Format:** Candid conversation with growth operators and funded founders
**Topics:** Real-world challenges, channel insights, automation wins
**Facilitator Role:** Help surface questions from your startup team. Listen for insights that connect to their specific situation.

**Panel Question Bank (suggested):**
- What mistakes do you see earliest founders make?
- Speed versus pixel-perfect -- where's the line?
- Social capital versus venture capital -- how to build momentum without money
- NDAs at idea stage -- helpful or harmful?
- Fundraising reality: investors invest in lines, not dots -- how should founders prepare months ahead?
- Staying human as a founder -- boundaries, wellness, transparency with teams
- What AI-powered workflow has created the most leverage for you?
- What channel worked for you that most people overlook?
- What did you try that failed spectacularly and what did you learn?

---

#### Session 2.2: Content Generation Workflow
**Duration:** ~60 minutes
**Goal:** Build a working automation workflow for content creation and distribution.

**What Founders Are Doing:**
1. Mapping workflow: Trigger (schedule, RSS, manual) -- AI generation -- formatting -- multi-platform publish
2. Setting up N8N or Zapier nodes: trigger, AI content node, formatting, output to LinkedIn/email/etc.
3. Testing end-to-end with a real example
4. Troubleshooting and iterating

**Facilitator Prompts:**
- "What's the smallest version of this that works?"
- "Can you test this today with real data?"
- "What breaks if you don't touch it for a week?"
- "How will you know if the content is good?"

**What to Watch For:**
- Founders building overly complex workflows before testing the simplest version
- Technical issues blocking progress -- have backup examples ready, or simplify the workflow
- Content workflows disconnected from the ICP and channel strategy from Block 1

**Tools:** N8N, Zapier, OpenAI or Claude API keys, social media APIs

---

#### Session 2.3: Lead Generation & Nurture System
**Duration:** ~60 minutes
**Goal:** Automate lead magnet delivery and a multi-touch nurture sequence.

**What Founders Are Doing:**
1. Mapping the full flow: Form submission -- lead magnet delivery -- CRM entry -- email sequence trigger
2. Building N8N or Zapier workflow with form webhook, email delivery, CRM integration, and sequence trigger
3. Writing the first 3 emails of the nurture sequence (value-first, low-pitch)
4. Testing with a real email

**Facilitator Prompts:**
- "What happens if they don't open email 1?"
- "How do you move them from awareness to consideration?"
- "What's your call-to-action in email 3?"
- "Can this run for 100 leads without you touching it?"

**What to Watch For:**
- Nurture sequence that pitches too early -- push for value-first in email 1 and 2
- Missing error handling in the workflow -- what happens when a form submits but the CRM is down?
- Email copy that sounds like automation -- should feel like a human wrote it

**Tools:** N8N, Zapier, Typeform, Fillout, Attio, Brevo, SendGrid, Claude for email writing

---

### Block 3: Build & Validate (2:30PM - 5PM)

---

#### Session 3.1: Sales & Support Workflow Automation
**Duration:** ~45 minutes
**Goal:** Build one high-leverage workflow for sales or support.

**Two Options (founder chooses based on current bottleneck):**

**Option A -- Sales Lead Enrichment:**
Form submission -- Clay/Hunter.io enrichment -- CRM update -- Slack notification

**Option B -- Support Ticket Automation:**
Email/form -- AI classification -- ticket creation -- auto-response

**What Founders Are Doing:**
1. Choosing sales or support based on current bottleneck
2. Mapping workflow nodes
3. Building in N8N or Zapier with basic error handling
4. Testing with real scenarios

**Facilitator Prompts:**
- "What manual task takes you 2 or more hours per week?"
- "Where do leads or tickets fall through the cracks?"
- "Can this workflow handle edge cases gracefully?"
- "How will you monitor failures?"

**What to Watch For:**
- Founders who want to automate before they've validated the manual process works
- Over-engineering (too many nodes) before proving the basic flow
- No monitoring or alerting -- automation that fails silently is worse than no automation

**Tools:** N8N, Zapier, Clay, Attio, Slack

---

#### Session 3.2: Analytics & Optimization Setup
**Duration:** ~30 minutes
**Goal:** Define metrics and create a simple dashboard for tracking progress.

**What Founders Are Doing:**
1. Defining their North Star Metric (qualified leads per week, activation rate, content engagement)
2. Identifying 3-5 supporting metrics
3. Building a simple dashboard (Google Sheets + Zapier/N8N, Plausible, or Mixpanel)
4. Planning their first A/B test for the 30-day period

**Facilitator Prompts:**
- "What's the one number that proves growth?"
- "How often will you check this dashboard?"
- "What triggers a pivot versus staying the course?"
- "Can you explain your metrics to a 10-year-old?"

**What to Watch For:**
- Vanity metrics -- push for metrics that predict revenue, not just traffic or impressions
- Dashboard so complex it won't be used -- simpler is always better
- No decision triggers defined -- "we'll see how it goes" is not a plan

**Tools:** Google Sheets, N8N for data pulls, Plausible, Google Analytics

---

#### Session 3.3: 30-Day Rapid Validation Plan
**Duration:** ~30 minutes
**Goal:** Create a week-by-week tactical action plan with clear decision points.

**Format:**
- Week 1: Deploy workflows, publish initial content, drive first traffic
- Week 2: Optimize based on early data, fix broken workflows, iterate messaging
- Week 3: Scale what's working, pause what's not, test new variations
- Week 4: Review metrics, decide pivot vs. persist, plan next 30 days

**Each week should have:**
- 3-5 specific actions (not vague)
- Success criteria ("50 landing page visitors," "10 email signups")
- Decision trigger ("If fewer than 5 signups, revisit the offer")

**Facilitator Prompts:**
- "What's realistic given your current time constraints?"
- "What would make you kill this experiment?"
- "Who is accountable for each action?"
- "How will you celebrate small wins?"

**What to Watch For:**
- Plans that are too ambitious for a founder with existing responsibilities
- Week 4 that just says "review" with no decision criteria
- No ownership assigned for each action

**Tools:** Notion, Google Doc template, Airtable

---

#### Session 3.4: Presentations & Cohort Feedback
**Duration:** ~30 minutes
**Goal:** Each startup presents their system to the cohort for peer feedback and accountability.

**Format:**
- 3-5 minutes per startup
- Show what you built, what your 30-day plan is, and one thing you're still uncertain about
- Facilitators and peers give pointed feedback: one thing that works, one sharpening question

**Facilitator Role:**
- Help your startup sharpen their presentation to be concrete, not vague
- Surface the "so what" if their presentation stays at the feature level
- Draw out the insight from peer feedback that is most actionable

## 6. Facilitation Principles

### Psychological Safety
Create the conditions for honest thinking before pushing for honesty.
- Set the stage early: "There are no bad ideas in divergence."
- Use "Yes, and..." explicitly -- it signals to the room that exploration is safe.
- Acknowledge that being a founder is hard and that uncertainty is expected.

### Provocateur Mindset
Ask the hard questions founders avoid:
- "What's your real unfair advantage in this channel?"
- "How will you measure success in 30 days?"
- "Why would someone give you their email for this lead magnet?"
- "What's your customer acquisition cost? Lifetime value?"
- "Why would someone choose you over doing nothing?"
- "Can this workflow run without you for a week?"

**Challenge-Response Patterns:**
- When a founder gives a vague answer: "Give me a specific example."
- When a founder says "it depends": "Walk me through the most likely case."
- When a founder deflects: "That's interesting -- but what do YOU think?"
- When a founder is stuck: "What's the smallest next step? Just one thing."

### Diverge Before Converging
Do not let teams converge too early. Push for alternatives before evaluating options. A team that picked the first idea they had did not explore well enough.

### Speed Over Perfection
Working code, working workflows, working prototypes beat planning documents. If a team is still planning at 2pm, intervene.

## 8. Post-Event Follow-Up

These are optional but strongly encouraged. The best facilitators stay connected to the founders they worked with.

### Week 1
- Follow up with your assigned startup to check initial progress
- Offer to answer technical questions or troubleshoot workflows
- Celebrate quick wins (with the founder's permission)

### Week 4 (30-Day Check-In)
- Schedule a 30-minute call or async check-in
- Review metrics and progress against the 30-day plan from Session 3.3
- Provide guidance on next steps or pivots
- For founders hitting Green on their validation gates, recommend the **Growth Operations skills** in the founder repo (see below)

### Week 8 (60-Day Check-In)
- Final structured check-in
- Collect success stories and testimonials
- Connect founders who might benefit from knowing each other

### Solo Mode

After the workshop, founders should switch their `config.json` to solo mode:

```json
{
  "facilitated_mode": false
}
```

This removes facilitator challenge prompts and makes skills more streamlined for independent use. Mention this during your Week 1 follow-up.

**Note on community:** Do not reference specific paid communities or platforms in your follow-up communications. Keep follow-up organic and relationship-based.
