# Session Pacing Guide

Use this guide to keep both tracks on schedule. Times are guidelines -- the lead facilitator may flex the agenda based on cohort momentum.

---

## Product Track

**Total structured time:** ~315 minutes (5h 15m) across activities and commitments.

---

### Activity 1: SWOT + Battle Cards (90 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Intro & SWOT | 0:00-0:15 | Facilitator frames competitive landscape. Founders fill in SWOT assumptions before research. |
| Competitor Research | 0:15-0:45 | Identify 3-5 competitors including status quo. Use Perplexity, GPT, Claude, Crunchbase. |
| Battle Card Generation | 0:45-1:15 | Build battle cards in Miro: name, positioning, ICP, pricing, onboarding, strengths, weaknesses, "how we win." |
| Review & Synthesis | 1:15-1:30 | Revisit SWOT with new evidence. Identify top 2-3 opportunity areas. |

**If running long, cut:** Compress review to 5 min. Carry synthesis into Activity 2 framing.

**Key facilitator prompts:**
- "What job is the customer hiring today without you?"
- "Where are switching costs the highest?"
- "What's your unfair advantage the big players can't copy soon?"

---

### Activity 2: Hypothesis Design (75 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Framing | 0:00-0:10 | Introduce "If ___ then ___ by ___" format. Show 1-2 examples. |
| Diverge | 0:10-0:35 | Generate 5-8 hypothesis options. No filtering yet. Push quantity. |
| Evaluate | 0:35-0:55 | Score each hypothesis: effort vs. impact. Sense-check against capabilities. |
| Converge | 0:55-1:15 | Select top 1-2 hypotheses. Document rationale. Write final "If / then / by" statements. |

**If running long, cut:** Shorten diverge to 15 min (aim for 4-5 options minimum). Combine evaluate + converge.

**Key facilitator prompts:**
- "Is this testable in 30 days?"
- "What would prove this wrong?"
- "Which hypothesis unlocks the most if it works?"

---

### Activity 3: Prototype Launcher (120 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Type Selection | 0:00-0:15 | Choose prototype type: service/journey map, GTM, pricing/packaging, sales story, or feature mock. Match to hypothesis. |
| Thought Starters | 0:15-0:30 | Review thought-starter options. Narrow to one build target. |
| Build | 0:30-1:45 | Build the prototype. Use Miro, sticky notes, Carrd.co, Lovable.dev, Figma, or AI generation. |
| Review | 1:45-2:00 | Internal review. What does this prototype test? What would a "pass" look like? |

**If running long, cut:** Shorten thought-starters to 5 min. Scope the build to the minimum viable artifact (e.g., a 3-slide story instead of a 10-slide deck).

**Prototype options (quick reference):**
- Service/journey: Map onboarding to first value; identify friction to remove
- GTM: Cold outreach + 5-step nurture, landing page, offer test
- Pricing/packaging: Tiers, usage metric, CAC/LTV quick math
- Sales story: 6-8 slide narrative for partners or prospects
- Feature: Clickable mock or AI-generated demo, not production code

**Tools:** Miro, Carrd.co, Lovable.dev, Replit, Figma, GPT/Claude/Perplexity

---

### Commitments (30 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Validation Planner | 0:00-0:15 | Each founder writes 3 commitments: 2 days / 2 weeks / 2 months. Specific actions, not intentions. |
| Pitch Prep | 0:15-0:30 | Prepare 3-minute share-out: hypothesis, prototype, top commitment. |

**If running long, cut:** Validation planner to 10 min. Pitch prep is informal -- no polish needed.

## Growth Track

**Total structured time:** ~270 minutes (4h 30m) across three blocks.

---

### Block 1: Define & Discover (90 min)

#### Session 1: ICP Definition (30 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| JTBD framing | 0:00-0:05 | Introduce Jobs-to-be-Done. "When _____, I want to _____, so I can _____." |
| Segment identification | 0:05-0:20 | Identify 2-3 distinct customer segments. Score by: pain intensity, willingness to pay, accessibility. |
| ICP selection | 0:20-0:30 | Commit to one primary ICP for the day. Document the rationale. |

**Key facilitator prompts:**
- "Who has this problem the worst?"
- "Where does this person spend their time online?"
- "What's the trigger event that makes them look for a solution?"

---

#### Session 2: JTBD Deep Dive (20 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Pain mapping | 0:00-0:10 | Map ICP's functional, emotional, and social jobs. Where does the current solution fail? |
| Opportunity framing | 0:10-0:20 | Identify 2-3 highest-leverage opportunity areas based on pain intensity. |

**If running long, cut:** Combine with ICP session. Pull the best insight and move on.

---

#### Session 3: Competitive Growth Analysis (40 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Competitor identification | 0:00-0:10 | Identify 3-5 direct and adjacent competitors. Include status quo. |
| Channel analysis | 0:10-0:25 | Use SEMrush, Ahrefs, or SimilarWeb. Analyze top keywords, content gaps, backlinks, paid strategy. |
| Battle card synthesis | 0:25-0:35 | Build competitive growth battle cards: strengths, weaknesses, channels, content types, CTAs. |
| Opportunity identification | 0:35-0:40 | "Where can we win that they're not focused on?" |

**If running long, cut:** Compress channel analysis to 10 min. Pull top 2-3 insights and move to battle cards.

**Deliverables:** ICP Canvas, Competitive Growth Matrix, Channel Selection Map

---

### Block 2: Design + AI Panel (90 min)

#### Session 4: Lead Magnet Selection (25 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Pain point alignment | 0:00-0:08 | Identify biggest pain point or knowledge gap for ICP. What would they pay for if packaged right? |
| Format brainstorm | 0:08-0:18 | Brainstorm formats: template, checklist, calculator, audit, mini-course, swipe file. |
| Selection | 0:18-0:25 | Choose one that is both valuable and achievable in the session. Draft the outline. |

**Key facilitator prompts:**
- "What question does your ICP Google before finding you?"
- "Can you create this in the next 60 minutes?"
- "What format makes them say 'I need this now'?"

---

#### Session 5: Content & Automation Workflow (25 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Workflow mapping | 0:00-0:08 | Map: Trigger -> AI generation -> formatting -> multi-platform publish. |
| N8N build | 0:08-0:20 | Set up schedule trigger, OpenAI/Claude node, formatting, and output. |
| Test | 0:20-0:25 | Run end-to-end with a real example. Capture what breaks. |

**If running long, cut:** Stub out the workflow structure without full testing. Move test to Session 7 block.

---

#### Session 6: Lead Magnet Delivery & Email Sequence (25 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Flow mapping | 0:00-0:08 | Map: Form submission -> lead magnet delivery -> CRM -> email trigger. |
| Email writing | 0:08-0:20 | Draft first 3 emails: value-first, low-pitch. AI-assisted. |
| First email review | 0:20-0:25 | Review email #1. Does it deliver on the lead magnet promise? |

**Key facilitator prompts:**
- "What happens if they don't open email #1?"
- "What's your call-to-action in email #3?"

---

#### AI Panel (15 min)

Candid conversation: what's working in AI-native growth in 2026. Real channels, real automation wins, honest failures.

**If running long, cut:** Compress to 10 min. Keep it focused on 2-3 practitioner insights.

**Deliverables:** Working Content Workflow, Lead Magnet Outline, Email Nurture Sequence

---

### Block 3: Build & Validate (90 min)

#### Session 7: Automation Build (35 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Choice | 0:00-0:05 | Choose: Sales lead enrichment OR support ticket automation. Based on current bottleneck. |
| Workflow build | 0:05-0:30 | Build in N8N with error handling. Map nodes, test end-to-end. |
| Edge case check | 0:30-0:35 | What breaks under edge cases? How does it fail gracefully? |

**Options:**
- Sales: Form submission -> Clearbit/Hunter.io enrichment -> CRM update -> Slack notification
- Support: Email/form -> AI classification -> ticket creation -> auto-response

**Key facilitator prompts:**
- "What manual task takes you 2+ hours/week?"
- "Can this workflow handle edge cases gracefully?"
- "How will you monitor failures?"

---

#### Session 8: North Star Metrics (25 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| North Star definition | 0:00-0:10 | Define the one number that proves growth (e.g., qualified leads per week, activation rate). |
| Supporting metrics | 0:10-0:18 | Identify 3-5 supporting metrics. Build simple dashboard in Google Sheets. |
| A/B test plan | 0:18-0:25 | Plan first A/B test for 30-day period. What are you testing and why? |

**Key facilitator prompts:**
- "What's the one number that proves growth?"
- "What triggers a pivot vs. staying the course?"
- "Can you explain your metrics to a 10-year-old?"

---

#### Session 9: 30-Day Plan (30 min)

| Block | Time | What's Happening |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Week-by-week planning | 0:00-0:18 | Week 1: Deploy + publish. Week 2: Optimize. Week 3: Scale what's working. Week 4: Review + decide. |
| Success criteria | 0:18-0:25 | Each week needs 3-5 specific actions and success criteria (e.g., "50 landing page visitors"). |
| Decision triggers | 0:25-0:30 | Define when to pivot vs. persist. "If <5 signups, revisit offer." |

**Key facilitator prompts:**
- "What's realistic given your current time constraints?"
- "What would make you kill this experiment?"
- "How will you celebrate small wins?"

**Deliverables:** Second Workflow, Analytics Dashboard, 30-Day Action Plan

---

## General Pacing Rules

- **Behind by 15+ minutes:** Cut the lowest-leverage diverge block. Move to convergence.
- **Ahead by 15+ minutes:** Add a "stress test" round -- ask one hard question per person.
- **Stuck team:** Re-center on ICP and one concrete next action. Escalate to lead facilitator if stuck for more than 10 minutes.
- **Energy crash:** Take a 5-minute break before afternoon block. Worth the time.
